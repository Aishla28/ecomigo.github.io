How climate works

‘Listen to the science. Before it’s too late’

The science is as solid as it gets - Greta Thunberg

The climate crisis is a complex challenge with deep roots. The stable climate of the Holocene era allowed human civilization to flourish, but the Industrial Revolution ushered in an era of environmental damage. Our activities have caused deforestation, extinction, and pollution, jeopardizing the planet's health. We've become accustomed to a degraded environment (shifting baseline syndrome), and the wealthy nations who caused most of the problem are not always held accountable. The effects of past emissions linger (carbon budget), and addressing the crisis requires transforming our economic systems and consumption habits. Scientists struggle to communicate the urgency effectively, and acknowledging the unequal burdens and historical responsibility can make solutions seem daunting. Our hunter-gatherer instincts make it difficult to grasp the slow-moving threat, but human ingenuity can solve this crisis if we work together with a sense of urgency and shared responsibility.
